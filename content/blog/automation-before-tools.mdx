## Document reality first

Write down what triggers the process, which information is required, who makes decisions, what can go wrong, and what a successful result looks like. The awkward exceptions are often more important than the happy path.

## Remove before you automate

Some steps exist only because an old process required them. Simplify, combine, or remove unnecessary work before introducing automation.

## Design the handoff

Every dependable automation needs clear ownership, useful logging, and a way for a person to intervene. That is what turns a clever demo into an operating system a business can trust.
